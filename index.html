Towards Robust Seismicity Characterization in Stable Continental Regions

A Bayesian framework for uncertainty-aware Gutenberg–Richter b-value estimation in Peninsular India.

Rashid Shams, Ravindra K. Gupta, and Mukat L. Sharma

Code & Data Citation

Overview

Stable Continental Regions such as Peninsular India are commonly viewed as tectonically stable, yet they have hosted damaging intraplate earthquakes, including the Koyna and Latur events. This project develops a Bayesian framework to estimate Gutenberg–Richter b-values while explicitly quantifying epistemic uncertainty.

The study compiles a homogenized and declustered earthquake catalogue for Peninsular India from 1950–2024, estimates regional and spatial b-values, and applies density-based clustering to identify coherent seismicity domains.

Methodological Workflow

1. Catalogue Compilation

Earthquake data were compiled from NCS, ISC, and USGS catalogues for Peninsular India and homogenized to moment magnitude Mw.

2. Declustering

Gardner–Knopoff, Uhrhammer, and Reasenberg declustering methods were tested. The GK-declustered catalogue was used for subsequent analysis.

3. Completeness Magnitude

Magnitude of completeness was evaluated using FMD, GFT, BIC, and bootstrapped sensitivity analysis. The adopted Mc is 2.7.

4. Bayesian b-value Estimation

A PyMC-based Bayesian framework with ADVI was used to estimate posterior distributions of b-values at regional, spatial, and cluster scales.

5. Spatial Clustering

DBSCAN and OPTICS were used to identify spatially coherent seismicity clusters, including Koyna, MH-GJ/Palghar, and Killari-Latur.

6. Depth-Resolved Analysis

Bayesian b-values were estimated across depth bins to examine depth-dependent seismicity variations within each cluster.

Key Results

  • The regional Bayesian b-value for Peninsular India is approximately 0.58 ± 0.02, consistent with MLE and bootstrap estimates.
  • Lower b-values are observed near tectonically active or reservoir-affected regions such as Koyna-Warna and Killari-Latur.
  • Higher b-values are observed in swarm-like seismicity zones such as the MH-GJ/Palghar region.
  • Bayesian posterior distributions provide credible intervals, enabling uncertainty-aware interpretation of b-value variability.
  • DBSCAN produced geologically coherent clusters that support cluster-based and depth-dependent seismicity analysis.
